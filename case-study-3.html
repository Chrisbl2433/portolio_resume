Beyond Banking — A 0→1 Investing Experience

A digital banking app wanted to expand beyond everyday banking and deposits into investing — a new growth pillar. But investing intimidates mainstream users, and the team didn't yet know whose needs to serve, which problems to solve, or what to build first. I led the 0→1 research that shaped the feature strategy — from understanding the space, to validating the experience before production, to monitoring it after launch.

Context

The app was strong at everyday banking and deposits and saw investing as its next pillar. But moving mainstream customers into investing is hard: low confidence, jargon, fear of loss, unclear needs. Before committing engineering to a roadmap, the team needed to know who to serve, what problems to solve, and what to build first. I led the research from a blank page.

My role

  • Lead researcher — ran the 0→1 programme end-to-end, discovery through post-launch.
  • Cross-functional partner — PM, PMM, Design, and the Engineering director, turning insight into a prioritised feature roadmap.

Approach

The programme ran as a continuous loop, from understanding the space through to learning from the live product.

Phase 1 · Generative discovery

In-depth interviews (n=12) plus a two-week diary study (n=10) to understand people's relationship with money and investing over time — the real problems and needs, and the barriers to starting.

Phase 2 · Concept testing

Concept testing (n=8) of candidate concepts to prioritise the roadmap against real user needs.

Phase 3 · Usability & launch

Iterative prototype and usability testing (n=6 per round) of the new features before production, then an app-review monitoring system to track reception and feed the next iteration.

Why pair pre-launch testing with review monitoring

Pre-production usability catches issues before they ship; review monitoring closes the loop, surfacing what real users hit at scale after launch. Together they make the 0→1 a learning loop, not a one-shot — so the roadmap keeps improving after release.

Findings

  • ✅Appetite was real, confidence wasn't. People wanted to grow their money but felt investing "wasn't for them" — the core problem was confidence, not capability.
  • 🧭Guidance beat control. Mainstream users valued guided, low-effort options over advanced trading tools.
  • 🔤Jargon was a barrier. Investing terminology alienated users; plain language and education were prerequisites, not nice-to-haves.
  • 🛡️Trust hinged on transparency. Clear fees, risk, and "what happens to my money" drove willingness to start.
  • 🎯This shaped the roadmap. Needs mapped to a first release focused on confidence, guidance, and transparency — deferring advanced tools.

Outcome & Impact

The research shaped the 0→1 investing strategy — defining the target users, the problems to solve, and a prioritised roadmap built around confidence, guidance, and transparency. It de-risked launch with pre-production usability and established an app-review monitoring loop for continuous post-launch learning. The guided, plain-language first release shipped as recommended, and among first-time investors it lifted activation and reduced early drop-off versus the original concept.
