Research That Compounds — Building the System Behind the Studies

Research was being commissioned faster than it could be reused. Studies got duplicated, findings vanished into old decks, and stakeholders kept asking me for research that already existed. I built the system that made research compound instead — a searchable source of truth for 23 studies across 12 markets, a quality bar to keep it trustworthy, and self-serve templates so anyone in the organisation could find and run good research themselves.

Turned 23 scattered studies across 12 markets into one searchable source of truth — with a peer-review quality bar and self-serve templates — so insight got reused across the whole organisation instead of rebuilt.

Context

As research scaled across 12 markets, output was growing but not compounding. The same questions got researched twice, hard-won findings were lost in scattered decks and drives, and stakeholders routinely pinged me to dig out a study from months earlier. Quality varied depending on who ran what. There was no single place to find what we already knew — so every study started closer to zero than it should have. I set out to fix the system, not just run more studies.

My role

  • Founder & owner — spotted the gap, made the case, and built the research operating system end-to-end.
  • Repository & taxonomy lead — designed the structure, tagging, and search that made insight findable.
  • Quality lead — established the peer-review bar and sign-off that kept the repository trustworthy.
  • Enablement & change driver — created the templates and training and drove org-wide adoption.

Approach

Three phases took the function from scattered outputs to a compounding, org-wide system.

Phase 1 · Audit & taxonomy

Audited the 23 existing studies, then designed a tagging taxonomy — by market, product area and journey stage, method, and insight theme — plus naming and quality standards so any finding could be located and trusted.

Phase 2 · Repository & quality bar

Structured the studies into one searchable source of truth at the insight level, not just whole decks; introduced a peer-review checklist and sign-off gate so only research that met the bar entered the repository.

Phase 3 · Democratise & sustain

Self-serve templates, a "how to run good research" playbook and training, and clear guardrails so anyone in the org could run lighter studies safely — with the review gate protecting quality.

Why a system, not just a repository

A repository on its own becomes a graveyard. Pairing it with a quality bar (peer review) and enablement (templates and training) is what makes insight actually get reused — and stay good as more people across the org start running research.

What I built

  • 📚A single source of truth. 23 studies across 12 markets, searchable at the insight level and findable in seconds.
  • 🏷️A shared taxonomy & standards. Consistent tagging by market, journey stage, method, and theme, with naming and quality conventions.
  • 🔍A peer-review quality bar. A review checklist and sign-off gate so everything entering the repository met a consistent standard.
  • 🧩Org-wide self-serve democratisation. Templates, a playbook, and training that let anyone in the org — PMs, designers, marketers — run good, safe research.
  • 🛡️Guardrails that protected quality. Lighter studies self-served; higher-stakes work (e.g. surveys, sampling) routed through review before publishing.

Outcome & Impact

The work turned 23 one-off studies into compounding, reusable knowledge — a genuine research operating system. Anyone in the organisation could find prior insight in seconds instead of re-commissioning it, which cut duplicated research and sped up new studies by starting from what we already knew. The peer-review bar standardised quality regardless of who ran the study, and self-serve templates extended good research org-wide without lowering the bar. Within two quarters, duplicated studies fell sharply and teams were self-serving prior insight for most new questions before commissioning fresh research — tracked via repository search and usage analytics.
