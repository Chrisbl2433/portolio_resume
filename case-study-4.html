Pioneering AI in Research — From a Live Co-Pilot to Synthetic Users

I build AI into research — not as a gimmick, but as infrastructure that makes the work faster and better. I've prototyped a live co-pilot that augments moderated sessions in real time, an agent that reviews and strengthens PRDs before research even starts, "synthetic users" for continuous testing, and LLM-accelerated synthesis. Always built to augment researchers, never replace them, with rigour intact.

0→1 AI research tooling — a live session co-pilot, a PRD-review agent, synthetic users, and LLM-accelerated synthesis — that raises the speed and quality of research across the team.

Context

Research demand outpaces research capacity: teams need insight faster, PRDs arrive with gaps, and testing cadence is limited by hands. I saw a chance to use AI to close those gaps — speeding up the work and raising its quality — without compromising rigour or compliance. So I built and piloted a set of AI research systems.

My role

  • Builder & founder — prototyped the tools 0→1 and shared them to raise research speed and quality.
  • Systems thinker — designed how the pieces fit the research workflow, not one-off hacks.
  • Guardian of rigour — kept human judgment, quality, and compliance central throughout.

Approach / principles

Augment, not replace

AI handles speed and scale; researchers keep judgment, framing, and the human read.

Systems, not hacks

Tools built to fit the end-to-end research workflow and be reused, not one-off experiments.

Rigour & compliance first

Every tool designed to protect data, privacy, and methodological quality.

How the live co-pilot works

It listens to a live session and surfaces real-time, cross-functional probing suggestions — so a single moderator can cover PM, Engineering, and Design angles in the moment, without breaking the conversation.

How the PRD-review agent works

It reads a draft product-requirements doc and checks it against a research-quality rubric — flagging gaps in problem framing, target users, success metrics, stated assumptions, and the areas that still need validation — so teams strengthen the thinking before a study is even scoped.

How the synthetic users were created

Grounded in patterns from real prior research, I built persona-based AI participants that respond and react in character. They're used for fast, low-cost dry-runs of tasks, stimuli, and discussion guides between rounds of real testing — pressure-testing the study design, never replacing real users. Findings are always validated with people.

What I built

  • 🤖Live research co-pilot. Real-time, cross-functional probing suggestions during moderated sessions.
  • 📝PRD-review agent. Reviews and strengthens PRDs before research starts — catching gaps in problem framing, success criteria, and UX validation.
  • 🧪Synthetic users. AI personas for continuous, low-cost feature testing between rounds of real research.
  • ⚡LLM-accelerated synthesis. Turns raw sessions into structured findings faster, freeing time for interpretation.

Outcome & Impact

The toolkit sped up synthesis, widened testing coverage, and raised PRD quality before research even began — while keeping human rigour firmly in the loop. In pilots it cut synthesis time on a typical study by roughly half and let the team pressure-test more concepts between formal rounds. The work established me as an AI-native researcher, setting a template for how research can adopt AI responsibly.
