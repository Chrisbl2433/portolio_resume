Pioneering AI in Research — Building AI-Assisted Tools Into the Research Workflow

I build AI into my research work, not as a novelty, but as a way to make the work faster and better. I've built AI-assisted support for live sessions, for strengthening research plans and questions before fieldwork starts, for early-stage concept testing, and for speeding up synthesis of qualitative data. Always built to augment researchers, never replace them, with rigour intact.

AI-assisted approaches across the research lifecycle, from live session support to synthesis, aimed at raising the speed and quality of research while keeping human judgment central.

Context

Research demand outpaces research capacity: teams need insight faster, plans arrive with gaps, and testing cadence is limited by hands. I saw a chance to use AI to close those gaps, speeding up the work and raising its quality, without compromising rigour or compliance. So I built AI-assisted tools across the research workflow.

My role

  • Builder & founder: prototyped the tools 0→1 and shared them to raise research speed and quality.
  • Systems thinker: designed how the pieces fit the research workflow, not one-off hacks.
  • Guardian of rigour: kept human judgment, quality, and compliance central throughout.

Approach / principles

Augment, not replace

AI handles speed and scale; researchers keep judgment, framing, and the human read.

Systems, not hacks

Tools built to fit the end-to-end research workflow and be reused, not one-off experiments.

Rigour & compliance first

Every tool designed to protect data, privacy, and methodological quality.

Why augment rather than automate

The goal was always speed and coverage, not replacing researcher judgment. AI support works best where it removes friction: helping a single moderator stay comprehensive in the moment, helping a plan get sharper before fieldwork starts, or turning raw sessions into structure faster. Every interpretive call stays with a human researcher, and every finding gets validated with real people.

What I built

  • 🤖Live session support. AI-assisted prompts that help a single moderator stay comprehensive during moderated sessions.
  • 📝Stronger research planning. AI-assisted review of research plans before fieldwork starts, catching gaps in framing and success criteria early.
  • 🧪Early-stage concept testing. AI-assisted approaches to pressure-test discussion guides and stimuli between rounds of real research.
  • ⚡Faster synthesis. Using LLMs to structure raw qualitative data faster, freeing more time for interpretation, with outputs validated against researcher-led synthesis before adoption.

Outcome & Impact

In use, these tools have delivered meaningfully faster synthesis, wider testing coverage, and stronger research plans going into fieldwork, while keeping human rigour firmly in the loop. They reflect how I think about adopting AI in research responsibly: as infrastructure that removes friction, not a shortcut around judgment.
